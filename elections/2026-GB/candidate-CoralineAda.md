## My Contributions to CHAOSS

Although I've admired the work that CHAOSS has been doing for a long time, I only got directly involved when the AI Alignment Working Group was announced in 2025. I've been an active contributor to the working group ever since, and just volunteered to co-chair with Emma Irwin. My most tangible contribution so far has been leading the design of an open-ended qualitative survey to understand the challenges and impact of AI/LLMs on the well-being of open source communities. I'm looking forward to continued collaboration on this timely and critical issue.

## Why I'm Running

I believe in community as one of the most fundamental but undervalued human technologies, and that community is all that protects the open internet from complete corporate capture. This belief has fueled a decades-long career working for the well-being of everyone who participates in open source. It's what inspired me to create the Contributor Covenant, now the gold standard open source code of conduct, and it's what inspires me to keep working for equity in spite of the current political backlash. 

I bring extensive experience in nonprofit leadership. I've worked with organizations including Bridge Foundry/RailsBridge (former board member), RubyTogether (former board member and treasurer), the Organization for Ethical Source (co-founder and executive director), the Governance Futures Network (founding board chair), and (m)otherboard (co-founder and god(m)other). Most recently, my focus has been on the design of open, participatory governance frameworks and collective decision-making protocols.

I'm deeply aligned with the heartfelt work that all of you do under the CHAOSS banner. I hope to leverage my skills and experience to help the CHAOSS community meet some of our inward- and outward-facing challenges in this time of rapid change and transformation.
