# How to Facilitate a CHAOSS Meeting

Some CHAOSS Working Group meetings have rotating facilitators to give people an opportunity to perform this role. Here’s a to-do list. Our meetings are very informal and casual, so don’t be nervous! 

## Before the Meeting

1. Make sure you follow up with the meeting schedule via our CHAOSS calendar. You may optionally add our calendar to your calendars or add the particular event to your personal calendar, so you don’t forget.
2.  Locate the ongoing Agenda google document for that Working Group in the calendar entry of the meeting of your choice. Every Working group uses one specific google document to record all the minutes of the previous and future meetings.
3.  Copy the Agenda and List of attendees from the last meeting and paste it at the top (under all the reference information). 
4. Change the date to the date of the meeting and clear the list of attendees to make room for the ones who will be attending. Leave some empty bullets so attendees know where to add their names.
5. Go through the last meeting's agenda to see if there are any AI (Action Items) from people. If so, leave them on the agenda for any updates. 
6. Add a reminder agenda item to pick next week’s facilitator.
7. If there’s nothing to put on the agenda, you can always add an item to discuss any open issues and Pull Requests as a group.
8. (Other people may add items to the agenda that they want to talk about, and that’s ok! You don’t need to know about everything on the agenda)

## During the Meeting

1. We usually start 2-3 minutes past the starting time to give people a minute to get settled. Whenever you are ready to start, Let the Zoom admin logged in as “CHAOSS Community” know. They will start recording the meeting and turn on Live Transcriptions.
2. Welcome everyone to the meeting. 
3. You can share your screen if you choose, so everyone is on the same page and looking at the same thing. You don’t have to do this though! 
4. Begin the meeting by discussing the agenda items one at a time. If you aren’t sure who added an item, you can just ask.
5. Sometimes we carve out meeting time for actually working on a metric. We may stop the recording, and then all join the google doc to add our suggestions to improve and build out the doc. This lasts 5-15 minutes. 
6. Keep an eye on the time the meeting ends, and if the discussions get close to the end, let people know the time is almost up to allow for any final comments. The items on the agenda weren’t discussed due to time can always be pushed to the next meetings 
7. (Sometimes the meeting doesn’t need to go to the end if everything has been discussed already, and that’s ok!)

You’re finished!
